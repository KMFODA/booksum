{"name": "Chapter 9", "summary": "Siddhartha observes the river and realizes that every movement of the water is new.<PARAGRAPH>He is painfully hungry and finds the ferryman he met years before. The man's name is Vasudeva.<PARAGRAPH>Siddhartha asks for a ride across the river. He has no money to offer the ferryman, but asks if Vasudeva will accept his fine clothes. The ferryman is confused, but agrees.<PARAGRAPH>Siddhartha asks if can move in with Vasudeva and learn about the river as the ferryman's apprentice.<PARAGRAPH>Vasudeva graciously invites Siddhartha to stay in his home and offers him a meal.<PARAGRAPH>Siddhartha eats a simple plate of bread and mango and recounts his life to Vasudeva. He is astounded by the Ferryman's profound ability to listen.<PARAGRAPH>Vasudeva remarks that he cannot teach, but that he can help Siddhartha to learn from the river--to learn how to be devout and to listen.<PARAGRAPH>Vasudeva rarely speaks, but the men work hard together and focus on listening to the river.<PARAGRAPH>It says to them, \"Come in for a dip!\"<PARAGRAPH>More accurately, the river teaches the two men about life.<PARAGRAPH>For instance, one day Siddhartha realizes that the river exists only in the present: it is everywhere at once, upstream, downstream, at the sea, and at the source. Life is the same way. Siddhartha realizes that his existence as a boy and his existence as an old man are not separate. Everything has its existence in the present.<PARAGRAPH>In the voice of the river Siddhartha hears the holy word \"Om\" being repeated over and over again.<PARAGRAPH>Vasudeva and Siddhartha live joyously on the river, listening to its stories and finding silent companionship in one another. Travelers begin mistaking the two for brothers.<PARAGRAPH>One day Siddhartha learns from traveling monks that Gotama Buddha is gravely ill and will die.<PARAGRAPH>Meanwhile, Kamala has joined the followers of the Buddha and is making a pilgrimage to see the holy man. She rests on the riverbank with her son, who is tired and wants to eat. While resting, Kamala is bitten by a snake.<PARAGRAPH>Vasudeva hears her scream and brings her home.<PARAGRAPH>Siddhartha immediately recognizes Kamala .<PARAGRAPH>He also realizes that the boy is his son.<PARAGRAPH>They clean her wound but she is badly poisoned. Siddhartha calms his crying son.<PARAGRAPH>When she regains consciousness, Kamala recognizes Siddhartha but sees how profoundly he has changed.<PARAGRAPH>She dies before she is able to tell Siddhartha that finding him was as good as it would have been to find Gotama Buddha.<PARAGRAPH>Siddhartha sits with Kamala for a long time after she dies. Later he sits awake and listens to the river. While he suffers, the river offers him a sense of unity.
