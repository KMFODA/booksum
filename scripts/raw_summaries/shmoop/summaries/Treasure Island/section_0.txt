When he hears that business is slow, he decides to stay at the inn.<PARAGRAPH>Even though the man's clothes are ragged, his manner is proud and commanding. He tells Jim's father he is a captain, but he won't say much more about himself.<PARAGRAPH>He spends all day looking out at the sea through his telescope and spying on the few sailors who pass through the area.<PARAGRAPH>The captain takes Jim aside and promises to pay him an allowance each month if Jim will help him keep an eye out for a one-legged sailor.<PARAGRAPH>The captain tells terrible stories about the violent life on the high seas, which terrify and fascinate all the men of the neighborhood.<PARAGRAPH>The captain keeps staying at the Admiral Benbow inn long after his money runs out.
